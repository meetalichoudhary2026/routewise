The problem

Commuting in Indian cities is still a daily obstacle course

Urban mobility is fragmented across metro, buses, autos, walking, and informal last-mile systems. Existing maps show routes, but not the lived reality of Indian commutes.

🚇

The commuter pain is real

Metro timing uncertainty. Bus arrival mismatch. Auto stand chaos. Last-mile unpredictability. People don’t just lose time — they lose energy, reliability, and peace of mind before the workday even begins.

“It’s not just about getting there. It’s about arriving as a human, not a victim of the commute.”

Broken Multimodal coordination

Today’s commute spans 2–4 transport modes, but decisions are still made using fragmented, low-context information.

Static Existing routing data

Schedule-based suggestions do not capture real delays, crowding, transfer friction, or local variability across Indian cities.

High Employer productivity leakage

Late arrivals, uncertain commute times, and exhausted employees reduce workplace readiness and overall workday productivity.
